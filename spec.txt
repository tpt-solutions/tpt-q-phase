Design Document: TPT Q Phase
Project Name: TPT Q Phase (Open-Source Quantum Control Systems)
Industry: Quantum Computing & Post-GPU AI
Status: Phase 1 Design / Pre-Alpha
1. Executive Summary
Quantum computers are the next leap beyond GPUs for AI. But the physical hardware of a quantum computer (dilution refrigerators, microwave pulse generators, cryogenic wiring) requires highly specialized, proprietary control software from IBM, Google, and Rigetti. These systems are closed-source, locked into specific hardware, and cannot be audited for security or optimized for custom algorithms.
TPT Q Phase is an open-source, memory-safe quantum control stack written in Rust. It handles the deterministic, microsecond-precision microwave pulses required to control qubits. It breaks the hardware lock-in of quantum giants and enables a new generation of quantum-AI hybrid systems.
2. System Architecture
Core Components:
The Pulse Sequencer (Rust): A bare-metal Rust runtime that generates microwave pulses with nanosecond precision. It executes quantum gate sequences (Hadamard, CNOT, etc.) by precisely timing the amplitude, frequency, and phase of microwave signals.
The Cryogenic Control Layer: Software that manages the dilution refrigerator, monitoring temperatures at each stage (300K, 4K, 100mK, 10mK) and adjusting heaters to maintain stable quantum operating conditions.
The Qubit Calibration Engine: An AI-driven system that characterizes qubit properties (T1 relaxation time, T2 coherence time, gate fidelity) and automatically tunes the pulse parameters to maximize performance.
The Quantum-Classical Interface: A high-speed API that allows classical AI models (running on GPUs in TPT DataCenter) to submit quantum circuits, receive results, and integrate quantum sampling into hybrid AI workflows.
The Error Correction Layer: Implementation of surface codes and other quantum error correction protocols. It monitors qubit states in real-time and applies corrective pulses to mitigate decoherence.
Target Tech Stack:
Pulse Sequencer Language: Rust (bare-metal, no_std, deterministic timing with <10ns jitter)
Cryogenic Control: Rust (for real-time temperature monitoring and heater control)
FPGA Integration: VHDL or Verilog for the FPGA firmware that generates the actual microwave pulses (Rust generates the FPGA configuration)
Classical Interface: Go or Python (for the API layer that classical AI models use to submit quantum circuits)
Quantum Frameworks: Qiskit (IBM), Cirq (Google), or custom Rust-based quantum circuit simulator
Hardware: Custom microwave pulse generators, arbitrary waveform generators (AWGs), dilution refrigerators (Bluefors, Oxford Instruments)
3. Integration with TPT Ecosystem
TPT DataCenter (Compute): Houses the classical computers that control the quantum hardware and run hybrid AI models
TPT Lithos (Chip Design): Designs the custom quantum control chips (AWGs, mixers) that TPT Q Phase uses
TPT Fulcrum (AI Engine): Can use quantum sampling to optimize the Knowledge Graph extraction process
The Link: Q Phase is the bridge between classical AI (GPUs) and quantum AI. It allows TPT Fulcrum to leverage quantum speedups for specific optimization problems.
4. Market Disruption
Quantum Startups: Can build quantum computers without being locked into IBM or Google's proprietary control stacks
Hybrid AI Systems: Can integrate quantum sampling into classical AI workflows for optimization, drug discovery, and materials science
Academic Research: Universities can teach quantum computing using open-source tools instead of proprietary academic licenses
National Security: Governments can build sovereign quantum infrastructure without relying on foreign-controlled proprietary systems
